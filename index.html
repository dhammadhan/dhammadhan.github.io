The dharma
of building
wealth.

Dhamma, in the Pali tradition, means right order — the principle that a life lived with clarity and intention produces outcomes that are both just and enduring. Dhamma Dhan applies this idea to wealth: not merely accumulation, but allocation with purpose.

Most portfolios are built by accident — assets gathered in response to moments, not to meaning. This framework insists otherwise. Each of the seven buckets occupies a deliberate place in the spectrum, from the immediately accessible to the generationally patient, from certainty to conviction.

Ahimsa — Do no harm
Violet and Indigo protect first. Stability is not timidity — it is the foundation that allows everything else to grow.
Viveka — Discernment
Blue, Green, and Yellow are distinguished by horizon and conviction, not risk alone. Know what you own and why.
Aparigraha — Non-grasping
Orange gold stores value without speculation. Wealth preserved is not wealth hoarded — it is wealth respected.
Seva — Service beyond self
Red hard assets outlast a single lifetime. The final bucket is not for you alone — it is what you leave behind.
