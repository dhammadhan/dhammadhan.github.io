Building Your Baskets

A practical framework to organize your financial life across the seven baskets. Begin with your daily reality—expenses and dependents—then build safety layers, then move toward growth and legacy.

Basket 1: Foundation

  1. Identify monthly expense. Your baseline outflow for household, food, utilities, and essential commitments.
  2. Identify annual expense. One-time costs: insurance, travel, education, maintenance.
  3. RD for annual expenses. Set up Recurring Deposits to cover each annual obligation.

Basket 1: Liquidity Layers

  1. 1 month liquidity in Savings. Immediate access for daily surprises and small emergencies.
  2. 6 months runway in FD. Fixed Deposits to cover medium-term uncertainties (job loss, illness).
  3. 6 months runway in Liquid Fund. Quick-access funds that preserve capital while earning a modest return.

Baskets 2–3: Safety & Compounding

  1. EPF/PPF monthly outgo. Automate contributions to your government-backed retirement accounts.
  2. Gov Bonds. Direct holdings of sovereign debt for stability and tax efficiency.
  3. Gilt Mutual Funds / SSY. For longer durations, gilt funds or Sukanya Samriddhi for dependents.

Baskets 4–7: Growth & Legacy

  1. Core growth portfolio. Index funds and balanced funds aligned to your time horizon.
  2. Conviction positions. Direct equity or private equity where your expertise creates an edge.
  3. Gold and real assets. Inflation hedge and generational wealth for your heirs.

Dhamma, in the Pali tradition, means right order — the principle that a life lived with clarity and intention produces outcomes that are both just and enduring. Dhamma Dhan applies this idea to wealth: not merely accumulation, but allocation with purpose.

Each of the seven baskets occupies a deliberate place in the spectrum, from the immediately accessible to the generationally patient, from protection to conviction. They align not only with time horizon and risk, but with the seven dimensions of a life lived well: self and family, security, relationships, social responsibility, spiritual work, preservation, and legacy.

Build with intention. Hold with dharma.
